## 📌 Today's 8 biggest stories

### 1. 🇪🇺 EU forces Google to open Android to AI competitors

The European Commission issued a binding decision under the Digital Markets Act (DMA) requiring Google to:

- Open Android to third-party AI assistants, providing **cross 11 Android functional groups** with voice activation and cross-app capabilities
- Share **anonymized search ranking, query, click, and browsing data** on FRAND terms

Timeline: search data sharing starts **January 2027**; Android interoperability takes effect **July 2027**.

Google's President of Global Affairs Kent Walker pushed back, arguing these decisions "could endanger the privacy and security protections of hundreds of millions of European users."

**Why it matters**: This directly attacks Google's two moats — the default install on 2 billion Android devices and two decades of accumulated search behavior data.

**Market reaction**: Alphabet stock dropped ~4% on the day.
